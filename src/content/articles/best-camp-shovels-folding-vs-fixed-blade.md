A good camp shovel is essential for responsible backcountry hygiene and can be surprisingly useful for tasks like snow clearing around your tent in winter. But choosing the right one depends on your priorities: weight, durability, and intended use. This article compares folding and fixed-blade camp shovels, focusing on their strengths and weaknesses for digging latrines and moving snow.
---
## Folding Camp Shovels: Packability and Versatility

Folding shovels prioritize packability. Their compact size makes them appealing for backpacking and situations where space is at a premium. However, this comes with trade-offs in durability and digging efficiency.

### Pros of Folding Shovels

*   **Packability:** The most significant advantage. Folding shovels collapse to a fraction of their extended size, fitting easily into a backpack.
*   **Versatility:** Many folding shovels offer multiple functions, such as a pickaxe or saw blade. These additions can be helpful in a pinch, but rarely perform as well as dedicated tools.
*   **Weight:** Generally lighter than fixed-blade shovels, although this can vary depending on the materials and construction. Look for models under 1.5 lbs for backpacking.

### Cons of Folding Shovels

*   **Durability:** The folding mechanism is inherently a weak point. Lower-quality models can break under stress, especially when digging in hard or frozen ground.
*   **Digging Efficiency:** The smaller blade size and shorter handle can make digging latrines or moving snow more time-consuming and strenuous.
*   **Complexity:** The folding mechanism adds complexity, potentially leading to malfunctions in harsh conditions.

### When to Choose a Folding Shovel

Folding shovels are a good choice when:

*   **Packability is paramount:** Backpacking, bikepacking, or any situation where space and weight are strictly limited.
*   **Occasional use:** If you only need a shovel for occasional latrine digging or light snow clearing.
*   **Versatility is desired:** If you value having multiple tools in one compact package.

## Fixed-Blade Camp Shovels: Durability and Performance

Fixed-blade shovels offer superior durability and digging efficiency compared to folding models. Their robust construction makes them ideal for demanding tasks and harsh environments. They can be a crucial part of your snow camping setup.

### Pros of Fixed-Blade Shovels

*   **Durability:** The absence of a folding mechanism makes them significantly stronger and less prone to breakage.
*   **Digging Efficiency:** Larger blade size and longer handle provide more leverage and allow you to move more material with each scoop. The shape of the blade can also improve efficiency; a wider, flatter blade is better for snow, while a narrower, sharper blade is better for digging in dirt.
*   **Ergonomics:** The longer handle typically provides a more comfortable and ergonomic digging experience, reducing strain on your back and arms.

### Cons of Fixed-Blade Shovels

*   **Packability:** The primary drawback is their fixed size, which can make them difficult to pack, especially in smaller backpacks.
*   **Weight:** Generally heavier than folding shovels, although some lightweight fixed-blade models are available.
*   **Specialized Use:** Fixed-blade shovels are primarily designed for digging, lacking the multi-tool functionality of some folding models.

### When to Choose a Fixed-Blade Shovel

Fixed-blade shovels are the better choice when:

*   **Durability is essential:** Camping in rocky terrain, frozen ground, or areas with heavy snow.
*   **Digging efficiency is a priority:** Regularly digging latrines, building snow shelters, or moving large amounts of snow.
*   **Comfort is important:** If you anticipate extended digging sessions, the ergonomic design of a fixed-blade shovel will be appreciated.

## Choosing the Right Camp Shovel: Key Considerations

Beyond the folding vs. fixed-blade decision, consider these factors when selecting a camp shovel:

*   **Blade Material:** Aluminum is lightweight and rust-resistant, but can bend or dent under heavy use. Steel is stronger but heavier and more prone to rust. High-carbon steel offers a good balance of strength and weight.
*   **Handle Material:** Aluminum, steel, and fiberglass are common handle materials. Aluminum is lightweight, steel is strong, and fiberglass offers good vibration dampening.
*   **Handle Length:** Longer handles provide more leverage and reduce back strain, but shorter handles are more compact. Choose a length that suits your height and intended use. Aim for a handle length of at least 16 inches for efficient digging.
*   **Blade Size and Shape:** A larger blade moves more material, but requires more effort. A narrower, pointed blade is better for digging in hard soil, while a wider, flatter blade is better for moving snow.
*   **Weight:** Consider the shovel's weight in relation to your overall pack weight. Every ounce counts, especially on long backpacking trips.
*   **Price:** Camp shovels range in price from budget-friendly to high-end. Consider your budget and intended use when making your selection.

## Latrine Etiquette and Snow Shelter Considerations

Regardless of which shovel you choose, remember to follow Leave No Trace principles when disposing of human waste. Dig a cat hole 6-8 inches deep and at least 200 feet (70 big steps) away from water sources, trails, and campsites. Cover and disguise the waste after use.

For snow shelters, a sturdy, larger shovel is essential. Snow caves and trenches require moving significant amounts of snow, and a flimsy shovel will quickly become frustrating. Consider a fixed-blade avalanche shovel, even if you're not in avalanche terrain. They're designed for moving large volumes of snow quickly and efficiently, and their durable construction can withstand the rigors of snow shelter construction.

# Nalgene 32oz — The Baseline Trail Bottle for Good Reason

The Nalgene 32oz has been the trail standard for 40 years for reasons that haven't changed: it's
nearly indestructible, weighs 3.2 oz, costs $13, and holds liquid without leaking regardless of
temperature. Everything else in this category is a trade-off against one of these properties.

## Tritan vs. original polycarbonate

The original Nalgene used polycarbonate (BPA). The current Tritan copolyester is BPA-free and
retains the same clarity, impact resistance, and temperature range (−40°F to 212°F for brief
boiling water contact). This makes it usable for pouring near-boiling water directly.

## Weight comparison

Nalgene 32oz: 3.2 oz. Hydro Flask 32oz insulated: 13.6 oz (4.25× heavier). Platypus DuoLock 1L
collapsible: 1.1 oz. The Nalgene sits in the middle — heavier than collapsibles, much lighter
than insulated steel. For temperatures above freezing where insulation isn't needed, the Nalgene
is the best weight-per-durability option.

## Filter threading

The 63mm wide mouth accepts Sawyer Squeeze, BeFree, and Katadyn filter threads directly — no adapter
needed. This makes it the default choice when building a gravity filter system.
